Our Philosophy

The Urasai Story

Since 2008, Urasai has been a sanctuary for authentic Japanese cuisine in the heart of the city.

Harmony in Every Detail

Urasai, meaning "serenity" in Japanese, embodies the quiet elegance and profound depth of traditional Japanese culinary arts. Our restaurant is designed as a tranquil escape from the bustling city outside.

We honor the centuries-old traditions of kaiseki (seasonal tasting menu) and edomae sushi, while incorporating subtle contemporary touches that highlight the natural beauty of each ingredient.

Every element—from the handcrafted tableware to the seasonal ikebana arrangements—is thoughtfully curated to create a harmonious dining experience that engages all the senses.

Master Chef

Hiroshi Yamamoto

Third-Generation Sushi Master

Born in Tokyo and trained for over 25 years in the rigorous traditions of Japanese cuisine, Chef Yamamoto brings a lifetime of expertise to Urasai. He apprenticed under legendary sushi master Jiro Ono before developing his own distinctive style.

Chef Yamamoto personally selects each fish at the market every morning and prepares all ingredients with meticulous attention to detail. His philosophy centers on shun (seasonality) and umami, creating dishes that capture the essence of each moment.

"True mastery lies not in complexity, but in revealing the inherent beauty of each ingredient," says Chef Yamamoto.
